William G. Faris: Research Interests

My research interests are in mathematical physics and in applied probability. Among the topoics:

  • The Feynman path integral of quantum mechanics
  • The theory of self-adjoint operators acting in Hilbert space
  • Stochastic partial differential equations
  • Neural networks
  • Wave propagation in random media
  • Equlibrium statistical mechanics
  • Cluster expansions

My writings also include books on a number of other subjects, including:

  • Logic (an approach through proof theory)
  • Vector fields and differential forms (with pictures)
  • Real analysis
  • Martingales
